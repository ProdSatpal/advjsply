Ultimate Guide to Sale Deed Registration in Nagpur (2026)

April 5, 2026 Adv. Jasvinder Singh Ply
6 min read

Buying property in Nagpur—Maharashtra's rapidly expanding city—is a landmark moment. However, ensuring your ownership is legally foolproof requires navigating state laws and local regulations. The **Sale Deed** (locally known as **Kharedikhat**) is the most crucial document that transfers legal rights from the seller to the buyer.

1. Pre-Purchase Legal Verification

Before you sign anything, verify the property's health:

  • 7/12 Extract (Satbara): The foundation of land ownership in Maharashtra. It shows the history of the land, cultivation, and any active disputes.
  • Title Deed Chain: Review ownership history for the last 30 years to avoid old claims.
  • Encumbrance Certificate (EC): Confirms the land is free from mortgages or bank loans.
  • NMRDA/NIT Approvals: Ensure the layout is sanctioned by Nagpur Metropolitan Region Development Authority or Nagpur Improvement Trust.

2. 2026 Stamp Duty Rates in Nagpur

Area Total Stamp Duty Composition
Nagpur Urban (NMC/NIT) 6% 4% Base + 1% LBT + 1% Metro Cess
Nagpur Rural (NMRDA) 4% No LBT/Metro Cess in most rural zones
Special Concession 5% 1% discount if registered in a woman's name

Registration Fee: 1% of the property value (capped at ₹30,000).

3. The Registration Process (7 Easy Steps)

  1. Drafting the Deed: Using a professional 'Kharedikhat' format that complies with the Maharashtra Ownership Flats Act (MOFA).
  2. Payment of Charges: Calcualte and pay stamp duty online via the **GRAS Portal**.
  3. Booking Slot: Use the IGR Maharashtra e-Stepin portal to book a time at the Sub-Registrar's Office (SRO).
  4. Physical Execution: Buyer, seller, and 2 witnesses visit the SRO.
  5. Biometric Verification: Fingerprints and photographs are captured.
  6. Witness Signatures: Witnesses confirm the transaction.
  7. Mutation (Ferfar): The final step—submitting the registered deed to the Tehsil or NMC to update your name on the 7/12 extract.

Nagpur mein Sale Deed (Kharedikhat) Registry: Puri Jankari

April 5, 2026 Adv. Jasvinder Singh Ply
6 min read

Nagpur jaise tezi se badhte shehar mein plot ya flat lena har kisi ka sapna hota hai. Lekin kanooni baato ko dhyan mein rakhe bina investment karna khatarnak ho sakta hai. **Sale Deed** (Marathi mein **'Kharedikhat'**) wo sabse zaruri kanooni dastawej hai jo property par aapka haq pakka karta hai.

1. Property Kharidne se pehle kya check karein?

  • 7/12 (Satbara Utara): Maharashtra mein zameen ke ownership ka sabse bada saboot. Isme land type aur purane malikon ki list hoti hai.
  • Chain of Documents: Pichle 30-40 saal ke purane registry documents check karein.
  • NMRDA aur NIT Approvals: Kya aapka layout sanctioned hai? Nagpur Improvement Trust (NIT) ya NMRDA ke zones check karna na bhulein.
  • MahaRERA: Naye projects ke liye developer ka MahaRERA registration check karein.

2. Nagpur mein Stamp Duty kitni lagti hai? (2026 Updates)

Nagpur mein stamp duty different zones mein alag hoti hai:

  • Nagpur Urban (NMC Limits): 6% Stamp Duty (jis mein 1% Metro Cess aur 1% LBT shaamil hai).
  • Nagpur Rural: 4% to 5% area ke mutabik.
  • Mahilaon ke liye Discount: Agar registry sirf ek mahila ke naam ho rahi hai, toh **1% ki choot** (concession) milti hai.
  • Registration Fees: Property ki value ka 1% (maximum ₹30,000 tak).

3. Sale Deed Registry ka Step-by-Step Process

  1. Drafting: Ek legal expert se sahi 'Kharedikhat' format taiyar karwayein.
  2. Online Payment: GRAS portal par apna Stamp Duty aur Registration Fee bharein.
  3. Appointment: IGR Maharashtra ki website par 'Slot Booking' karein.
  4. SRO Visit: Buyer, Seller aur 2 Gawahon (Witnesses) ke sath Registar office jayein.
  5. Fingerprints aur Photos: Office mein biometric verification hoga.
  6. Registry Proof: Document scan hone ke baad aapko scan copy turant mil sakti hai.
  7. Ferfar (Mutation): Registry ke baad 7/12 par apna naam chadhwana na bhulein.

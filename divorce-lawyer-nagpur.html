Frequently Asked Questions

What are the grounds for contested divorce?

Under the Hindu Marriage Act, you can initiate a contested divorce on several robust grounds. These include cruelty (mental or physical), adultery, desertion (continuous for at least two years), conversion to another religion, mental disorder, or renunciation. We thoroughly evaluate your unique situation to construct a compelling case.

Can I get a divorce without spouse’s consent?

Yes. If your spouse refuses a mutual agreement, you have the absolute legal right to file for a contested divorce based on specific statutory grounds such as cruelty, desertion, or adultery. This process demands substantial evidence and an aggressive litigator, which is exactly where our expertise lies.
